# Roadmap

Use this file when the user wants to connect one paper to the next, avoid random topic hopping, or define a research line.

## Principle

A roadmap is not a list of unrelated papers. It is a controlled sequence of steps toward a stable problem direction.

Each next project should emerge from one of the previous project's boundaries:

- coverage boundary
- robustness boundary
- efficiency boundary
- mechanism boundary

## Roadmap Step Template

Describe each step with:

- long-term target
- current subproblem
- evidence the subproblem is real
- why it blocks the long-term target
- proposed next step
- smallest experiment to justify the step

## Good Continuity

Continuity is strong when the next step:

- reuses most of the code, data, or evaluation stack
- sharpens a limitation already observed
- strengthens the central scientific story
- can be explained as a natural consequence of the prior result

Continuity is weak when the next step:

- changes task, data, and objective at once
- is driven mostly by trend-chasing
- lacks evidence that the previous limitation matters
- cannot inherit tools or intuition from the previous project

## Boundary Review

After finishing a project, write a one-page boundary review:

- Core claim:
- Conditions where the claim holds:
- Conditions where the claim fails:
- Suspected reason for failure:
- What is engineering debt:
- What is research debt:
- Most natural next step:
- Two experiments to test that step:

This review should be written before starting the next project.

## Selection Rule

When multiple next-step candidates exist, rank them by:

- importance
- continuity
- testability

Prefer the step with the highest combined score, not the most fashionable topic.
